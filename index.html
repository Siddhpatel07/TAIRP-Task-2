Sahi Paneer

Ingredients

  • 250g paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked and ground to a paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add cashew paste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add the paneer cubes and mix gently.
  8. Add heavy cream and garam masala. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Garlic Naan

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, baking soda, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add yogurt, milk, and oil. Knead into a soft dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 2 hours.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into oval shapes.
  5. Sprinkle chopped garlic on top and press it gently into the dough.
  6. Heat a skillet over high heat and cook the naan until bubbles appear. Flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  7. Brush with melted butter and sprinkle with fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Serve hot with Sahi Paneer or any curry of your choice.
